# The Secret Strike

## Lesson Brief

- **Series:** Adventures in the Bible
- **Subject/Theme:** Sin brings consequences, but repentance brings deliverance.
- **Bible Focus:** Israel's sin, Moab's oppression, and God's deliverance through Ehud.
- **Main Passage:** Judges 3:12-30
- **Main Point:** Sin always brings trouble, but God is merciful to deliver sinners who turn back to Him.

## Memory Verse

> **Proverbs 28:13**
> 
> He that covereth his sins shall not prosper: but whoso confesseth and forsaketh them shall have mercy.

## Lesson Outline

### 1. Israel Forgot God And Chose Sin

> **Judges 3:12-14**
> 
> And the children of Israel did evil again in the sight of the LORD: and the LORD strengthened Eglon the king of Moab against Israel, because they had done evil in the sight of the LORD. And he gathered unto him the children of Ammon and Amalek, and went and smote Israel, and possessed the city of palm trees. So the children of Israel served Eglon the king of Moab eighteen years.

Israel was still a very young country at this point in history. They hadn't even had a king yet but they were instead ruled by God through the priests. Sometimes the people didn't listen to God and when they didn't listen to God, they would get in trouble and God would have to send people called judges to come and help get them out of trouble. 

Judges 3 says, "the children of Israel did evil again in the sight of the LORD." That word "again" is important. This was not the first time Israel had wandered from God. Sin may look exciting or easy at first, but it always pulls people away from God and toward trouble.

Many times during this period of Israel's history, they would get into trouble. While they were in trouble, they had a lot of problems. This would cause them to get right with God. They would repent and ask God to forgive them of their sins and they would turn away from their sins and start doing right again. When this happened God would send a judge to come and fix their problems and then they would live in peace and happiness for a while until they started to do wrong again. 

In our story today Israel was being naughty and they were being oppressed by a country called Moab that was led by a king named Eglon.

**Practical Application for Children:** Children can also know what is right and still choose wrong "again." They may disobey after being warned, lie after being corrected, or go back to a bad attitude after saying they were sorry. God wants children to see sin honestly instead of treating it like a game.

**Illustration:** Bring a backpack with a few books inside. Ask a child to hold it, then keep adding books while saying, "One more wrong choice will not matter." Soon the backpack becomes heavy. Sin may start with "just one" choice, but it becomes a heavy burden and brings consequences.

### 2. Israel Cried Unto The LORD

18 years is a long time for anyone to live as a slave, to be oppressed by someone else, to be treated badly, to have your things taken without being paid for, to be forced to work harder than everybody else for very little in return.

During these long 18 years the Israelites began to remember who God was and what God expected from them.  they remembered the goodness of God and that if they repented from their sin and turned back to Him and did right, He would deliver them.

> **Judges 3:15**
> 
> But when the children of Israel cried unto the LORD, the LORD raised them up a deliverer, Ehud the son of Gera, a Benjamite, a man lefthanded: and by him the children of Israel sent a present unto Eglon the king of Moab.

Finall, the children of Israel cried unto the LORD. The passage does not give a long speech from Israel, but it does show that they finally turned their cry toward God. They could not rescue themselves from Moab. They needed God to deliver them.

God did not owe Israel another rescue, but He is merciful. When His people cry to Him, He hears them and forgives them. Sin brings consequences, but God is not cruel. He wants sinners to turn to Him.

> **1 John 1:9**
> 
> If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness.

**Practical Application for Children:** When a child sins, the right response is not hiding, blaming, laughing, or pretending it did not happen. The right response is to come honestly to God, admit the sin, ask forgiveness, and turn back to what is right.

**Illustration:** Ask the children what they would do if they were being bullied by a bigger kid. They would call for help. Israel was stuck in trouble they could not fix. Their cry to God was like saying, "LORD, we need You. We cannot save ourselves."

>  it's important to remember that God is not sitting up in heaven ready to punish everyone who disobeys Him right away. God gives us a lot of mercy and grace. He has many chances to confess and repent and to turn from our sin. God wants us to do right but if we continue to do wrong over and over and over again, at some point He has to allow judgment so that we will learn.

### 3. God Raised Up An Unexpected Deliverer

> **Judges 3:15-20**
> 
> But when the children of Israel cried unto the LORD, the LORD raised them up a deliverer, Ehud the son of Gera, a Benjamite, a man lefthanded: and by him the children of Israel sent a present unto Eglon the king of Moab. But Ehud made him a dagger which had two edges, of a cubit length; and he did gird it under his raiment upon his right thigh. And he brought the present unto Eglon king of Moab: and Eglon was a very fat man. And when he had made an end to offer the present, he sent away the people that bare the present. But he himself turned again from the quarries that were by Gilgal, and said, I have a secret errand unto thee, O king: who said, Keep silence. And all that stood by him went out from him. And Ehud came unto him; and he was sitting in a summer parlour, which he had for himself alone. And Ehud said, I have a message from God unto thee. And he arose out of his seat.

God raised up a man named Ehude, who was left-handed. Ehud was not described as a mighty warrior or famous king or intimidating mand. He was a normal person, just like you or me, but God chose him for this moment.

God likes to use normal people to do great things. He does that because it shows that he is the person that actually did the miracle not the person. God can use any of us if we let Him but the important part is to make sure that we live for God and do things for His glory and don't do things for people to see us and see what we can do. 

God had a plan to deliver the Israelites from King Eglon and the Moabites. The Israelites had to pay a special tax to the Moabites called a tribute. Ehud was the person that was supposed to bring it to King Eglon.  Ehud prepared the tribute but also prepared a special surprise: he made a two-edged dagger and hid it under his clothes.  Ehud then travels to King Eglon and presents the tribute to him. Ehud then leaves but turns back with a secret message. Eglon thinks he is about to hear something special for himself, so he has everyone else leave  so he can hear the special message himself.

**Practical Application for Children:** Children may think, "I am too young," "I am not important," or "God cannot use me." Ehud reminds us that God can use people others might not expect. The point is not how impressive we are; the point is whether we are available to obey God.

**Illustration:** Hold up an ordinary pencil. It is small and not very exciting by itself, but in the hand of a good artist or writer it can make something useful. Ehud was not the hero because he was impressive by himself. God used him as the deliverer.

### 4. Ehud Faced The Enemy With Courage

> **Judges 3:21-22**
> 
> And Ehud put forth his left hand, and took the dagger from his right thigh, and thrust it into his belly: And the haft also went in after the blade; and the fat closed upon the blade, so that he could not draw the dagger out of his belly; and the dirt came out.

When Eglon stood up, Ehud struck him with the hidden dagger. Ehud was alone with the powerful enemy king. The doors were shut. The servants were nearby. If Ehud was caught, he would die. But God was with Ehud and He wanted to deliver His people from Eglon and the Moabites.

Ehud was courageous not because he had training or was a great warrior or because he was brave but because he was doing the work God had raised him up to do.  Ehud was simply following God's instructions and he knew that if he followed God's instructions, God would bless his mission and protect him.

And because he obeyed, even though it was scary and he might die, his people were freed from the Moabites and the oppression that they were living under.  the Israelites got to live happy and prosperous lives again not just because of the work that Ehud had done but because they repented of their sins. They decided to stop doing wrong and decided to obey and do what God wanted them to do.

**Practical Application for Children:** Courage does not mean a person never feels afraid. Courage means doing what is right because God is greater than the danger. Children can show courage by telling the truth, obeying God when friends laugh, admitting when they are wrong, or standing for what is right even when it is hard.

### 6. Ehud Sounded The Trumpet And God Gave Rest

> **Judges 3:23-30**
> 
> Then Ehud went forth through the porch, and shut the doors of the parlour upon him, and locked them. When he was gone out, his servants came; and when they saw that, behold, the doors of the parlour were locked, they said, Surely he covereth his feet in his summer chamber. And they tarried till they were ashamed: and, behold, he opened not the doors of the parlour; therefore they took a key, and opened them: and, behold, their lord was fallen down dead on the earth. And Ehud escaped while they tarried, and passed beyond the quarries, and escaped unto Seirath. And it came to pass, when he was come, that he blew a trumpet in the mountain of Ephraim, and the children of Israel went down with him from the mount, and he before them. And he said unto them, Follow after me: for the LORD hath delivered your enemies the Moabites into your hand. And they went down after him, and took the fords of Jordan toward Moab, and suffered not a man to pass over. And they slew of Moab at that time about ten thousand men, all lusty, and all men of valour; and there escaped not a man. So Moab was subdued that day under the hand of Israel. And the land had rest fourscore years.

After Ehud struck Eglon, he escaped. The servants waited outside, thinking Eglon was resting. While they waited, Ehud escaped to Seirath.  but his mission wasn't over. If he had stopped there, the Moabites still would have oppressed Israel. They would have recovered from their dead king and would have punished Israel greatly.

Instead of stopping there, Ehud blew a trumpet in the mountain of Ephraim and called Israel to follow him. The people followed him and defeated Moab. Israel had rest, happiness, and prosperity for eighty years. Their sin had brought eighteen years of oppression, but God's mercy brought peace and rest.

**Practical Application for Children:** God can bring deliverance that people cannot bring for themselves. Go to God when you are wrong, trapped, afraid, or under the weight of sin. God may not remove every consequence, but He is able to forgive, help, restore, and lead them back to peace.

## Segue Into The Plan Of Salvation

Israel's story shows us something true about all of us. Israel sinned against God, and their sin brought consequences. We have sinned too. Romans 3:23 says, "For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God." Sin is not only a problem for nations in the Old Testament. Sin is in our hearts.

Israel needed a deliverer from Moab, but we need a greater Deliverer from sin. We cannot save ourselves by being nicer, trying harder, or promising never to do wrong again. Romans 6:23 says, "For the wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord."

God sent Jesus to be the Savior. Jesus never sinned. He died on the cross for our sins, was buried, and rose again the third day. 1 Corinthians 15:3-4 says that Christ died for our sins, was buried, and rose again according to the scriptures.

When Israel cried unto the LORD, God sent deliverance. When a sinner calls on the Lord Jesus Christ, God saves. Romans 10:13 says, "For whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved." Repentance is not hiding sin or excusing sin. It is turning honestly to God. Believe on Jesus Christ, and He will save you.

## Main Applications For The Kids

- Sin is serious and always brings consequences.
- Do not hide sin, excuse sin, or keep going back to sin "again."
- Instead, seek God's forgiveness and mercy and turn from your sin.
- God can use unexpected people who are willing to obey Him.
- Courage means doing what is right because God is greater than the danger.
