# The Tent Peg Victory

## Lesson Brief

- **Series:** Adventures in the Bible
- **Subject/Theme:** God delivers His people and uses courageous obedience.
- **Bible Focus:** Israel's oppression under Jabin and Sisera, Deborah's message from God, Barak's battle, and Jael's courageous choice.
- **Main Passage:** Judges 4-5
- **Main Point:** God wants us to trust His Word and stand on His side because He is greater than every enemy.

## Possible Lesson Titles

- The Tent Peg Victory
- Jael And Sisera
- When God Fought For Israel

## Memory Verse Options

- **Psalm 20:7** - "Some trust in chariots, and some in horses: but we will remember the name of the LORD our God." This fits because Sisera trusted his chariots, but God gave Israel the victory.
- **Deuteronomy 31:6** - "Be strong and of a good courage..." This fits because Barak and Jael both needed courage when the enemy looked strong.
- **Judges 5:31** - "But let them that love him be as the sun when he goeth forth in his might." This fits the end of Deborah's song and reminds children that those who love the LORD should shine for Him.

## Lesson Outline

**Quick Review From Last Week:** Last week we learned about Ehud and Eglon from Judges 3. Israel had sinned against God, and their sin brought trouble. Moab conquered Israel and oppressed them for eighteen years. When Israel repented of their sin and cried unto the LORD, God raised up Ehud, an unexpected deliverer, and used him to free Israel from King Eglon and the Moabites.

After that victory, the land had rest for eighty years. That means many children grew up hearing how God had delivered Israel. They served God and obeyed Him for a long time but as we discussed last week they eventually fell back into sin. Even though God gave them many chances to do right, they continued to ignore Him and so they got in trouble.

### 1. Israel Turned From God And Fell Into Trouble Again

> **Judges 4:1-3** And the children of Israel again did evil in the sight of the LORD, when Ehud was dead. And the LORD sold them into the hand of Jabin king of Canaan, that reigned in Hazor; the captain of whose host was Sisera, which dwelt in Harosheth of the Gentiles. And the children of Israel cried unto the LORD: for he had nine hundred chariots of iron; and twenty years he mightily oppressed the children of Israel.

Judges 4 begins with the sad words that Israel "again did evil in the sight of the LORD." The cycle in Judges kept repeating: the people forgot God, chose sin, and fell into trouble.

This time the enemy was Jabin, king of Canaan, and the captain of his army was Sisera. Sisera had nine hundred chariots of iron, which made him look almost impossible to defeat. But this time things were harder. The Bible says this time they were oppressed for 20 years instead of 18. The Bible also uses another word describing them as being mightily oppressed. So this was even worse than when Israel was oppressed by King Eglon of Moab.

**Practical Application for Children:** Sin never makes life better. It may promise fun or freedom, but it pulls people away from God and brings trouble. Children should learn to turn back to God quickly instead of waiting until sin has made a bigger mess.

**Illustration:** Talk about a dog who is fenced in his yard and wants to get out but doesn't realize that the fence is keeping him safe.

### 2. God Gave His Word Through Deborah

In verse 3 again we see that the children of Israel cried unto the Lord and repented of their sin again and turned back to Him. So God, in His mercy, plans for them to be saved from King Jabin.

> **Judges 4:4-10** And Deborah, a prophetess, the wife of Lapidoth, she judged Israel at that time. And she dwelt under the palm tree of Deborah between Ramah and Bethel in mount Ephraim: and the children of Israel came up to her for judgment. And she sent and called Barak the son of Abinoam out of Kedesh-naphtali, and said unto him, Hath not the LORD God of Israel commanded, saying, Go and draw toward mount Tabor, and take with thee ten thousand men of the children of Naphtali and of the children of Zebulun? And I will draw unto thee to the river Kishon Sisera, the captain of Jabin’s army, with his chariots and his multitude; and I will deliver him into thine hand. And Barak said unto her, If thou wilt go with me, then I will go: but if thou wilt not go with me, then I will not go. And she said, I will surely go with thee: notwithstanding the journey that thou takest shall not be for thine honour; for the LORD shall sell Sisera into the hand of a woman. And Deborah arose, and went with Barak to Kedesh. And Barak called Zebulun and Naphtali to Kedesh; and he went up with ten thousand men at his feet: and Deborah went up with him.


Deborah was a prophetess and a judge in Israel. The people came to her for leadership, and God used her to speak His message. Deborah called a man named Barak and told him that the LORD had commanded him to gather ten thousand men and go fight Sisera.

God's promise was clear: He would deliver Sisera into Barak's hand. But Barak was afraid to go unless Deborah went with him. Deborah agreed to go, but she told him the honor of the victory would not go to him. The LORD would sell Sisera into the hand of a woman.

One thing that's important to notice here is that God told Barak that He would deliver Sisera into his hand but Barak was scared. Because he was scared and needed someone else to go with him, God would no longer allow him to get the glory of the victory and instead gave it to someone else.

**Practical Application for Children:** When God says what is right, we should obey, right away, even if we feel nervous or scared. Courage does not mean we never feel afraid. Courage means we trust God enough to do what He says.

> **Joshua 1:9** Have not I commanded thee? Be strong and of a good courage; be not afraid, neither be thou dismayed: for the LORD thy God is with thee whithersoever thou goest.

**Illustration:** Ask the children to imagine a race: where there is a race car driver and a spotter. The spotter sits way up high where he can see the whole track and all the cars at one time. The race car driver can't. He can only see what's next to him and in front of him. He might think that driving one direction or making a particular move is the best move but the spotter is going to tell him something different. The race car driver needs to trust the spotter because the spotter can see more than the race car driver and knows things that the race car driver doesn't, even though the race car driver may think he knows what's best. We need to remember that God is better and smarter and can see more than the spotter in a race.

### 3. God Fought For Israel Against The Chariots

> **Judges 4:12-16** And they shewed Sisera that Barak the son of Abinoam was gone up to mount Tabor. And Sisera gathered together all his chariots, even nine hundred chariots of iron, and all the people that were with him, from Harosheth of the Gentiles unto the river of Kishon. And Deborah said unto Barak, Up; for this is the day in which the LORD hath delivered Sisera into thine hand: is not the LORD gone out before thee? So Barak went down from mount Tabor, and ten thousand men after him. And the LORD discomfited Sisera, and all his chariots, and all his host, with the edge of the sword before Barak; so that Sisera lighted down off his chariot, and fled away on his feet. But Barak pursued after the chariots, and after the host, unto Harosheth of the Gentiles: and all the host of Sisera fell upon the edge of the sword; and there was not a man left.

Sisera brought all of his chariots and all of his army to fight Israel. From the outside, Sisera looked stronger. He had iron chariots, trained soldiers, and years of power over Israel. But Deborah told Barak, "Up; for this is the day in which the LORD hath delivered Sisera into thine hand."

The battle belonged to the LORD. God is far more powerful than any soldier or army or warrior. Judges 4 says the LORD discomfited Sisera and all his chariots. That means God embarrassed Sisera. Look at the song the people sang after the victory:

> **Judges 5:21-22** The river of Kishon swept them away, that ancient river, the river Kishon. O my soul, thou hast trodden down strength. Then were the horsehoofs broken by the means of the pransings, the pransings of their mighty ones.

> **Psalms 20:7** Some trust in chariots, and some in horses: but we will remember the name of the LORD our God.

**Practical Application for Children:** Children may face problems that feel too big for them: fear, temptation, pressure from friends, or situations they cannot control. God is not weak when the problem is big. Trust Him, obey Him, and remember that He is greater than what scares you.

### 4. Sisera Ran From The Battle

> **Judges 4:17-20** Howbeit Sisera fled away on his feet to the tent of Jael the wife of Heber the Kenite: for there was peace between Jabin the king of Hazor and the house of Heber the Kenite. And Jael went out to meet Sisera, and said unto him, Turn in, my lord, turn in to me; fear not. And when he had turned in unto her into the tent, she covered him with a mantle. And he said unto her, Give me, I pray thee, a little water to drink; for I am thirsty. And she opened a bottle of milk, and gave him drink, and covered him. Again he said unto her, Stand in the door of the tent, and it shall be, when any man doth come and enquire of thee, and say, Is there any man here? that thou shalt say, No.

When God gave Israel the victory, Sisera left his chariot and ran away on foot. The strong captain with nine hundred chariots was now running and hiding. He came to the tent of a woman named Jael. Jael was a Kenite. The Kenites were friends with the Canaanites so Sisera thought he had found someone that would protect him.

Sisera thought he had found a safe hiding place. He asked for water, and Jael gave him milk. He asked her to stand at the door and lie if anyone came looking for him. Sisera was still trying to escape the judgment God had spoken, but no one can hide from the LORD.

### 5. Jael Chose The LORD's Side

> **Judges 4:21-24** Then Jael Heber’s wife took a nail of the tent, and took an hammer in her hand, and went softly unto him, and smote the nail into his temples, and fastened it into the ground: for he was fast asleep and weary. So he died. And, behold, as Barak pursued Sisera, Jael came out to meet him, and said unto him, Come, and I will shew thee the man whom thou seekest. And when he came into her tent, behold, Sisera lay dead, and the nail was in his temples. So God subdued on that day Jabin the king of Canaan before the children of Israel. And the hand of the children of Israel prospered, and prevailed against Jabin the king of Canaan, until they had destroyed Jabin king of Canaan.

While Sisera slept, Jael took a tent peg and a hammer and killed him. This mighty warrior fell at the hands of a lady taking care of her home. God didn’t need a great warrior. God could use anyone and God used Jael to end Sisera's cruelty.

Jael wasn't a warrior. Jael didn't have time to practice or prepare. Jael wasn't even a Jew but God brought her an opportunity to serve Him and she chose to serve God right away. Sometimes you're going to find yourself in a position where you all of a sudden have to choose right or wrong. You're not going to have a heads up to pray about it or to read the Bible or to ask someone for advice. It's going to be all of a sudden and you have to make a decision right away. Question is: Are you going to choose to obey and to serve God?

Jael is looked upon with great admiration and respect not because she was a great warrior with a sword, a spear, and a shield, but because she was a normal person who used the tools that she had to do a great thing for God. She didn't have training. She wasn't famous or rich or powerful. She just used milk and a hammer and brought a great victory for the Lord.

**Practical Application for Children:** Children are not soldiers, but they still face choices about which side they will stand on. Will they help someone lie, laugh at wrong, hide sin, or go along with disobedience? Or will they choose God's side and do what is right?

## Segue Into The Plan Of Salvation

Israel needed deliverance from Jabin and Sisera, but every person needs a greater deliverance from sin. Israel could not defeat Sisera's army by their own strength, and we cannot save ourselves by our own goodness. Romans 3:23 says, "For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God."

Sisera's chariots could not save him from God's judgment. Our good works, church attendance, family, or promises cannot save us from sin either. Romans 6:23 says, "For the wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord."

God sent Jesus to be the Savior. Jesus never sinned. He died on the cross for our sins, was buried, and rose again the third day. He is the true Deliverer who can save us from sin and make us God's children.

When God shows us our sin, we should not hide like Sisera or keep repeating Israel's cycle of disobedience. We should repent and believe on Jesus. Romans 10:13 says, "For whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved."

## Main Applications For The Kids

- Sin brings trouble, but God is merciful when people cry to Him.
- Trust God's Word even when the problem looks stronger than you.
- Courage means obeying God and standing on His side.
- Do not hide sin or help someone else hide wrong.
