# The Hunter Jesus Hunted Down

## Lesson Brief

- **Series:** Adventures in the Bible
- **Subject/Theme:** Jesus seeks and saves even the worst of sinners.
- **Bible Focus:** Saul's persecution of Christians and his conversion on the road to Damascus.
- **Main Passage:** Acts 7:54-60; Acts 8:1-3; Acts 9:1-22
- **Main Point:** No one has sinned too badly to be saved; Jesus came to seek and save sinners and can completely change anyone who believes on Him.

## Possible Lesson Titles

- The Hunter Jesus Hunted Down
- Stopped On The Damascus Road
- From Enemy To Preacher

## Memory Verse Options

- **1 Timothy 1:15** - "This is a faithful saying, and worthy of all acceptation, that Christ Jesus came into the world to save sinners; of whom I am chief." Paul used his own past to show how willing Jesus is to save sinners.
- **Luke 19:10** - "For the Son of man is come to seek and to save that which was lost." This captures the lesson's central truth: while Saul was hunting Christians, Jesus was seeking Saul.
- **Luke 5:31-32** - "They that are whole need not a physician; but they that are sick. I came not to call the righteous, but sinners to repentance." Jesus did not avoid sinful people; He came to call them to repentance.

## Lesson Outline

shortly after Jesus went back to heaven, the disciples went out and told people everywhere about everything that he had done, and lots of people got saved and became Christians. People started following God and obeying God. They went out and told others, and lots of people started believing.

this made some people very mad. They didn't like that people were following Jesus. and I want you guys to remember that when we decide to follow Jesus and do right, it's going to make some people mad. And some people aren't going to like us because of it. And they don't like it. Because when we follow Jesus, it shows them where their sin is and it makes them feel bad.

there was one man in particular who really hated the Christians. He would go around looking for Christians that he could throw in jail or have killed. this man was named Saul.

### 1. Saul Stood With The Men Who Killed Stephen

**Main Passage:** Acts 7:54-60; Acts 8:1

> **Acts 7:58**
>
> And cast him out of the city, and stoned him: and the witnesses laid down their clothes at a young man's feet, whose name was Saul.

> **Acts 8:1**
>
> And Saul was consenting unto his death. And at that time there was a great persecution against the church which was at Jerusalem; and they were all scattered abroad throughout the regions of Judaea and Samaria, except the apostles.

**Summary/Lesson:** Stephen, was a man who loved Jesus and boldly told the truth. some men got mad at the things that he was saying about Jesus, and they dragged him outside Jerusalem and threw stones at him until he died.

Nearby stood a young man named Saul. Saul did not throw the stones, as far as Scripture tells us, but he gave everyone permission to throw the stones. Acts 8:1 says that Saul was "consenting unto his death."

Stephen was killed for preaching Jesus, and Saul stood with the people who killed him.

### 2. Saul Hunted Christians From House To House

**Main Passage:** Acts 8:1-3

> **Acts 8:3**
>
> As for Saul, he made havock of the church, entering into every house, and haling men and women committed them to prison.

**Summary/Lesson:** Stephen's death was not enough for Saul. A great persecution began, and believers fled from Jerusalem. Imagine Christian families listening for footsteps outside their doors. Imagine parents trying to keep their children quiet when someone pounded on the door. Saul entered house after house, dragged away both men and women, and had them put in prison. could you imagine being at home and a man breaks down your door and grabs your parents and hauls them off to jail, just because you were a Christian?

The Bible says Saul "made havock of the church." He was trying to tear it apart. he didn't want people to go to church or ready their Bibles. In his later testimony, Saul said, "I persecuted this way unto the death, binding and delivering into prisons both men and women" (Acts 22:4). that means not only did he take them to prison, but he had them killed. He also said that he punished believers, tried to force them to blaspheme, and persecuted them and that he was "exceedingly mad at them". Saul was not merely confused or impolite. He was a violent enemy of Christ and His people. 

> Acts 26:11
> And I punished them oft in every synagogue, and compelled them to blaspheme; and being exceedingly mad against them, I persecuted them even unto strange cities.

### 3. Saul Set Out To Capture Even More Believers

**Main Passage:** Acts 9:1-2

> **Acts 9:1-2**
>
> And Saul, yet breathing out threatenings and slaughter against the disciples of the Lord, went unto the high priest, And desired of him letters to Damascus to the synagogues, that if he found any of this way, whether they were men or women, he might bring them bound unto Jerusalem.

**Summary/Lesson:** Saul heard that there were followers of Jesus in Damascus, a city more than one hundred miles away. That was not too far for him. He went to the high priest and asked for official letters giving him authority to arrest them. Then he started down the road with companions, determined to return with Christian men and women as prisoners.

the Christians there may have thought they were safe from Saul, because they were so far away. But Saul knew about them, and he was coming for them! Saul had authority in his hand, hatred in his heart, and the begging of Christians in his ear. He thought he was the hunter. But there was Someone Saul had not planned to meet on that road.

### 4. Jesus Stopped Saul On The Road

**Main Passage:** Acts 9:3-9

> **Acts 9:3-6**
>
> And as he journeyed, he came near Damascus: and suddenly there shined round about him a light from heaven: And he fell to the earth, and heard a voice saying unto him, Saul, Saul, why persecutest thou me? And he said, Who art thou, Lord? And the Lord said, I am Jesus whom thou persecutest: it is hard for thee to kick against the pricks. And he trembling and astonished said, Lord, what wilt thou have me to do? And the Lord said unto him, Arise, and go into the city, and it shall be told thee what thou must do.

**Summary/Lesson:** Just as Saul came near Damascus, a light from heaven suddenly shone around him. It was brighter than the midday sun.

> Acts 26:13
> At midday, O king, I saw in the way a light from heaven, above the brightness of the sun, shining round about me and them which journeyed with me.

Saul fell to the ground. Then he heard a voice call his own name: "Saul, Saul, why persecutest thou me?"

Saul asked, "Who art thou, Lord?" Saul knew exactly who was talking to him, otherwise he wouldn't have called him Lord. The answer must have struck him harder than the light: "I am Jesus whom thou persecutest." Jesus was alive. Stephen had told the truth. The Christians Saul arrested had told the truth. Saul had believed he was fighting a false religion, crazy people, but he had actually been fighting the risen Son of God. When Saul hurt Christ's people, Jesus took it personally: "Why persecutest thou me?"

Saul was coming to Damascus to find Christians, but Jesus found Saul first. Jesus knew every cruel thing Saul had done. He knew about Stephen, the homes, the prisons, the threats, and the killings. Yet Jesus did not say Saul was too wicked to save. Jesus stopped him, confronted him with the truth, and called him to a completely new life. Jesus didn't just want to save Saul, He wanted to give Saul a great mission:

> Acts 26:16-18
> But rise, and stand upon thy feet: for I have appeared unto thee for this purpose, to make thee a minister and a witness both of these things which thou hast seen, and of those things in the which I will appear unto thee; Delivering thee from the people, and from the Gentiles, unto whom now I send thee, To open their eyes, and to turn them from darkness to light, and from the power of Satan unto God, that they may receive forgiveness of sins, and inheritance among them which are sanctified by faith that is in me.

Saul arose from the ground, but he could not see. The powerful man who planned to capture christians and drag them back to Jerusalem now had to be led by the hand into Damascus. For three days he couldn't see or eat or drink. Saul had entered that road proud and threatening; he entered Damascus blind, humbled, and waiting on God.

**Practical Application for Children:** Jesus already knows every sin you have committed, including the sins no one else knows about. You do not need to hide from Him or try to make yourself good enough first. Jesus came looking for lost sinners. Admit the truth about your sin, turn to Him, and believe on Him to save you.

### 5. Jesus Sent Ananias To His Frightened Enemy

**Summary/Lesson:** In Damascus lived a disciple named Ananias. The Lord told him where Saul was and commanded him to go. Ananias knew Saul's reputation. He had heard how much evil Saul had done, and he knew Saul had come to arrest believers like him. He was scared that Saul would arrest him—but Jesus told him to go.

When Ananias entered the house, he did something incredible. He put his hands on the person that spent so much time and energy killing Christians and called him "Brother Saul." Jesus had a purpose for the man everyone else knew as an enemy. Saul would carry Christ's name to Gentiles, kings, and Israel. The man who had tried to silence the name of Jesus would spend the rest of his life proclaiming it.

**Illustration:** Ask how Ananias might have felt walking toward the house where Saul waited. With each imaginary step, repeat one thing Ananias knew: "He arrested Christians. He came for people like me. He has authority." Then read Jesus' words: "Go thy way: for he is a chosen vessel unto me."

### 6. The Enemy Of Jesus Became A Preacher Of Jesus

**Main Passage:** Acts 9:18-22

> **Acts 9:18-20**
>
> And immediately there fell from his eyes as it had been scales: and he received sight forthwith, and arose, and was baptized. And when he had received meat, he was strengthened. Then was Saul certain days with the disciples which were at Damascus. And straightway he preached Christ in the synagogues, that he is the Son of God.

**Summary/Lesson:** As soon as Ananias called him "Brother Saul" Saul could see again. He got up, was baptized, and spent few days with the very people he had intended to arrest and kill. Then he entered the synagogues (buildings like churches)—but this time not to hunt Christians. He preached that Jesus is the Son of God. Everyone who heard him were confused and amazed. They knew what Saul had done in Jerusalem and why he had come to Damascus.

Jesus did not merely improve Saul's manners or calm down his temper. Jesus gave him a new life, a new family, and a new purpose. Saul's past was still real, and he never pretended otherwise. Years later he called himself the chief of sinners, but he also joyfully said, "Christ Jesus came into the world to save sinners" (1 Timothy 1:15). Saul's terrible past became evidence of Christ's astonishing love and mercy.

Rip a piece of paper. The pieces cannot repair themselves. Explain that we cannot undo our sin or make our own hearts new. Jesus alone saves.

> 2 Corinthians 5:17
> Therefore if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new.

When Jesus came into saul's life Jesus made him a new person, a completely different person. **[Do the magic trick.]** Jesus can do the same thing in your life and in the life of anybody else.

## Segue Into The Plan Of Salvation

Saul's story is dramatic, but the reason he needed to be saved is the same reason every one of us needs to be saved. Saul had frightening sins in his past, while a child may think, "I have never put anyone in prison." But the Bible says, "For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God" (Romans 3:23). We have lied, disobeyed, acted selfishly, harbored anger, and chosen our way instead of God's way. Sin makes every person lost and separates us from God.

Jesus said, "They that are whole need not a physician; but they that are sick" (Luke 5:31). A sick person does not prove he is well before visiting a doctor; he goes because he needs help. Jesus did not come because people were already righteous and going to Heaven. He said, "I came not to call the righteous, but sinners to repentance" (Luke 5:32). We must stop defending our sin and agree with God that we need to be saved.

Jesus, the sinless Son of God, died on the cross for our sins and rose again. Saul later wrote, "Christ died for our sins according to the scriptures; And that he was buried, and that he rose again the third day according to the scriptures" (1 Corinthians 15:3-4). The risen Jesus who met Saul is alive today.

No life is so bad that the blood of Jesus cannot cleanse it. No child is so good that he does not need Jesus. Salvation is not earned by becoming nicer, getting baptized, going to church, or fixing everything first. "Believe on the Lord Jesus Christ, and thou shalt be saved" (Acts 16:31). Turn from trusting yourself and believe that Jesus died and rose again to save you. If you want to be saved, speak with a teacher after the lesson so someone can show you from the Bible how to trust Christ.

## Main Applications For The Kids

- **If you are not saved, come to Jesus today.** He already knows your whole history, He came to seek and save the lost, and He will save anyone who believes on Him.
- **Do not call anyone too bad for Jesus to save.** Pray for difficult people and, when it is safe and appropriate, tell them what Jesus has done.
- **If you are saved, live in your new direction.** You will not become perfect overnight, but you can confess sin, obey Christ, and let others see that Jesus is changing you.
- **Do not let past sin keep you from serving God.** Saul never bragged about his past, but he also did not let it stop him from obeying Christ. God's mercy is greater than your history.
