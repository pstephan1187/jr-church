# Adventures in the Bible

## General Direction

This summer series continues the same general direction as the previous Adventures in the Bible lessons: exciting Bible stories that show God working through real people in dangerous, unusual, or high-pressure moments.

The repeated emphasis should be that adventures with God are not about chasing excitement for ourselves. They come when people obey God, stand for truth, trust His Word, and live for His glory. Several lessons also give strong warnings: pride, rebellion, fear, jealousy, selfishness, and disobedience can turn a person's life into trouble very quickly.

Each lesson should keep the story vivid and concrete for the kids, but the application should stay plain: obey God, trust God, tell the truth, stand for right, and remember that only Jesus can save us from sin.

## Series Thread

- God uses bold witnesses who tell the truth even when it is costly.
- Jesus has power over storms, demons, sin, and changed lives.
- God can use unlikely people in unusual ways.
- Pride and rebellion lead to confusion, loss, and judgment.
- Faithful obedience may be dangerous, but God is always greater than the danger.
