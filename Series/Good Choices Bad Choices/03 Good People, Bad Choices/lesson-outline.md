# Good People, Bad Choices

## Lesson Brief

- **Series:** Good Choices Bad Choices
- **Subject/Theme:** Choose to please God, not people.
- **Bible Focus:** Ananias and Sapphira pretending to give everything while secretly keeping part back.
- **Main Passage:** Acts 5:1-11
- **Main Point:** When you live to please people, it leads to bad decisions. Living to please God will always lead to good decisions.

## Memory Verse

**Proverbs 29:25-26** - "The fear of man bringeth a snare: but whoso putteth his trust in the LORD shall be safe. Many seek the ruler's favour; but every man's judgment cometh from the LORD."

## Lesson Outline

### 1. A Good Gift Can Hide A Bad Heart

> **Acts 5:1-2**
> But a certain man named Ananias, with Sapphira his wife, sold a possession, And kept back part of the price, his wife also being privy to it, and brought a certain part, and laid it at the apostles’ feet.

Before Acts 5, many people in the church were giving to help others. Some sold things they owned. Some sold land or houses and brought the money to the apostles so people who needed help could be cared for. Barnabas was one real person who sold land and then gave the money he sold the land for.

Then Ananias and Sapphira sold something too. They wanted people to think they were giving a big, generous gift like the others. They wanted people to praise them too, but in their hearts they did not really want to give that much away.

Their problem was not that they kept some of the money. Peter made it clear that the land and money were theirs to choose what to do with. Their sin was pretending. They secretly kept part of the money, but told everyone that they had given all of the money. They lied so people would praise them for giving more than they really gave.

> **Matthew 6:1-4**
> Take heed that ye do not your alms before men, to be seen of them: otherwise ye have no reward of your Father which is in heaven. Therefore when thou doest thine alms, do not sound a trumpet before thee, as the hypocrites do in the synagogues and in the streets, that they may have glory of men. Verily I say unto you, They have their reward. But when thou doest alms, let not thy left hand know what thy right hand doeth: That thine alms may be in secret: and thy Father which seeth in secret himself shall reward thee openly.

**Practical Application for Children:** Children can be tempted to make what they did sound bigger or better than it really was. They might say they cleaned the whole room when they only picked up a few toys, say they did all the work when someone else helped, or act like they gave more than they really gave. God wants us to tell the truth instead of exaggerating to look better to other people.

### 2. Wanting People's Praise Can Lead To Lying

> **Acts 5:3-4**
> But Peter said, Ananias, why hath Satan filled thine heart to lie to the Holy Ghost, and to keep back part of the price of the land? Whiles it remained, was it not thine own? and after it was sold, was it not in thine own power? why hast thou conceived this thing in thine heart? thou hast not lied unto men, but unto God.

Peter asked Ananias why Satan had filled his heart to lie to the Holy Ghost. Ananias did not simply make a mistake with money; he chose deception. He wanted the church to believe something that was not true. He wanted his friends to think he was closer to God than he really was.

This is where doing things so people like you can become dangerous. When pleasing people matters most, we may start hiding, exaggerating, pretending, or lying so people will think good of us. A bad decision often begins when we care more about what people think than what God knows.

> **Proverbs 12:22**
> Lying lips are abomination to the LORD: but they that deal truly are his delight.

**Practical Application for Children:** Children may be tempted to lie so they do not get embarrassed, lose a game, disappoint a parent, or look bad in front of friends. The right choice is to tell the truth quickly, even when it is hard, because God already knows.

### 3. God Is In The Room

> **Acts 5:3-4**
> But Peter said, Ananias, why hath Satan filled thine heart to lie to the Holy Ghost, and to keep back part of the price of the land? Whiles it remained, was it not thine own? and after it was sold, was it not in thine own power? why hast thou conceived this thing in thine heart? thou hast not lied unto men, but unto God.

Peter told Ananias that he had not lied unto men, but unto God. This made the sin very serious. Ananias was acting as if the apostles and the church were the only ones watching, but God was present with His people.

Children need to learn that God is not only at church when we sing or pray. God sees the classroom, bedroom, playground, car, and home. He sees us and knows our thoughts no matter where we are or what we are doing. Remembering that God is always with us helps us choose truth even when no person would catch us.

> **Ecclesiastes 12:14**
> For God shall bring every work into judgment, with every secret thing, whether it be good, or whether it be evil.

**Practical Application for Children:** Before making a choice, instead of thinking "Will I get in trouble" or "Will other like me if I do this", pause and ask, "Would this please God?" That question can help you tell the truth, obey when you're all alone, and confess sin instead of hiding it.

### 4. Sin Is Serious, Even When It Looks Small

> **Acts 5:5**
> And Ananias hearing these words fell down, and gave up the ghost: and great fear came on all them that heard these things.

> **Acts 5:7-10**
> And it was about the space of three hours after, when his wife, not knowing what was done, came in. And Peter answered unto her, Tell me whether ye sold the land for so much? And she said, Yea, for so much. Then Peter said unto her, How is it that ye have agreed together to tempt the Spirit of the Lord? behold, the feet of them which have buried thy husband are at the door, and shall carry thee out. Then fell she down straightway at his feet, and yielded up the ghost: and the young men came in, and found her dead, and, carrying her forth, buried her by her husband.

When Ananias heard Peter's words, he fell down and died. Later, Sapphira came in and repeated the lie. Peter even asked her specifically if they sold the land for the amount they said and she lied to his face. She also died. This shows us how important it is to God that we are honest about what we do, why we do it, and that we don't tell lies! Telling lies is serious to God! They weren't even doing anything wrong, but because they lied about it, and were wanting people to pat them on the back instead of doing something to please God, they lied.

God was teaching the early church that sin, hypocrisy, and lying to God are not little things. The amount of money was not the issue. The issue was a heart willing to lie before God and His people. God is loving, but He is also holy.

> **Numbers 32:23**
> But if ye will not do so, behold, ye have sinned against the LORD: and be sure your sin will find you out.

**Practical Application for Children:** Children should not treat sin like a joke. A lie, a hidden wrong choice, or pretending to be better than they are should be confessed to God instead of protected. The good choice is to stop, tell the truth, and ask for forgiveness.

**Illustration:** Show a small stain on a clean white cloth. Even if the stain is small, it stands out because the cloth is clean. God's holiness shows us that sin is serious, even when we think it is small.

### 5. The Fear Of God Helps Us Make Good Choices

> **Acts 5:11**
> And great fear came upon all the church, and upon as many as heard these things.

After Ananias and Sapphira died, great fear came on all the church and on everyone who heard what happened. This fear was not just being scared of punishment. It was realizing that God is holy, and expects us to respect and obey Him.

The fear of God helps us live differently. Instead of living for to make others happy with us or to get attention or praise we remember that it is most important if we make God happy. You should understand that living to please God is always better than living to impress your friends, your teachers, or even your parents.

> **2 Corinthians 5:9**
> Wherefore we labour, that, whether present or absent, we may be accepted of him.

**Practical Application for Children:** A child can choose to please God by:
- telling the truth
- obeying quietly
- admitting wrong
- giving without showing off
- doing right even when friends laugh

These choices may not always make them popular, but they please God.

### 6. God Would Rather Have A True Heart Than A Big Show

> **Acts 5:4**
> Whiles it remained, was it not thine own? and after it was sold, was it not in thine own power? why hast thou conceived this thing in thine heart? thou hast not lied unto men, but unto God.

Ananias and Sapphira did not have to give all the money. Peter said, "Whiles it remained, was it not thine own? and after it was sold, was it not in thine own power?" They could have brought part of the money honestly and said, "This is what we are giving." That would have been plenty generous and far better than pretending to give everything when they didn't.

This helps us contrast their story with the widow's mite. The widow gave much less money than the rich people around her, but Jesus said she had given more because He saw her heart. Ananias and Sapphira wanted a big-looking gift with a false heart. The widow gave a small-looking gift with a true heart. God is not impressed by a show; He is pleased by faith, honesty, and love.

Contrast against the widow's mite.

> **Mark 12:41-44**
> And Jesus sat over against the treasury, and beheld how the people cast money into the treasury: and many that were rich cast in much. And there came a certain poor widow, and she threw in two mites, which make a farthing. And he called unto him his disciples, and saith unto them, Verily I say unto you, That this poor widow hath cast more in, than all they which have cast into the treasury: For all they did cast in of their abundance; but she of her want did cast in all that she had, even all her living.

You do not need to impress God or other people with big-looking actions. A small honest gift, a quiet act of obedience, or a truthful confession can please God more than a showy action done for attention.

**Practical Application for Children:** Sometimes children may think, “What I can do is not very much.” Maybe they cannot give much money, sing the loudest, answer every question, or help in a big way. But God is not looking for the biggest show. He sees the heart. A small act of obedience, a small gift, or a quiet right choice can please God when it is done honestly for Him.

## Segue Into The Plan Of Salvation

Ananias and Sapphira remind us that sin is not only the bad things people see. Sin is also in the heart. We can look good at church, say the right words, and even do the right things, but God still sees our sin. That is why none of us can save ourselves by looking good or making better choices.

Romans 3:23 says, "For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God." Romans 6:23 says, "For the wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord." That means our sin is serious, but God has made a way for sinners to be saved.

Jesus never lied, never pretended, and never sinned. He died on the cross for our sins, was buried, and rose again. 

> **1 Corinthians 15:3-4**
> For I delivered unto you first of all that which I also received, how that Christ died for our sins according to the scriptures; And that he was buried, and that he rose again the third day according to the scriptures:

John 3:16 tells us that "whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life."

We do not need to hide from God or pretend before Him. We need to come to Him honestly, admit that we are sinners, and believe on Jesus Christ to be saved. Acts 16:31 says, "Believe on the Lord Jesus Christ, and thou shalt be saved." Romans 10:13 says, "For whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved."

Good choices matter, but good choices cannot wash away sin. Only Jesus can save us. After we are saved, God helps us live to please Him.

## Main Applications For The Kids

- Do not do right things only so people will notice you.
- Tell the truth quickly, even when telling the truth is hard.
- Remember that God sees the heart, not just the outside action.
- Ask, "Will this please God?" before making a choice.
- God is pleased by an honest heart more than an impressive-looking gift.
- Confess sin instead of hiding it or pretending it is not there.
- Good choices do not save us. We need Jesus to save us from sin.
